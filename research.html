Climate-amplified energy insecurity modeling

Current research links outage risk, climate hazards, socioeconomic vulnerability, housing, and energy burden at tract scale.

Project site Related projects
Columbia
Jan 2026 - Present

Research Assistant

Columbia Climate School, Lamont-Doherty Earth Observatory

  • Assisted in developing a tract-level machine learning framework to quantify energy insecurity by integrating climate hazards, socioeconomic vulnerability, housing, and electricity outage datasets across New York City.
  • Built data pipelines harmonizing LEAD, FEMA National Risk Index, and energy-system datasets with more than 3 million rows to construct indicators such as energy burden and outage risk.
  • Conducted statistical analysis using regression and count models, seasonality analysis, and vulnerability testing across boroughs and years from 2014 to 2023.
  • Currently transitioning from statistical modeling into machine learning models for community-level outage risk prediction.
